# Sprint 1 — Criteria Alignment: Filled, per-category country polygons on the themed globe

Mode: criteria alignment (pre-build). Agreed acceptance criteria for Sprint 1 of
the `/travel` globe-first revamp. These supersede the DRAFT ACs in
`agent/BACKLOG.md` for Sprint 1. No implementation is prescribed; every criterion
is verifiable by interacting with the running site (screenshots, sampled canvas
pixels, DOM, console, frame-diff) at 1280×900 and 390×844.

## Verification notes (constraints the evaluator will honor; not ACs)

- The globe is a WebGL `<canvas>`. "Color" checks mean **sampled canvas pixels**
  (screenshot region color / `readPixels`), NOT `getComputedStyle` — the fills
  are not DOM elements.
- Theme re-coloring is driven by React state (`useTheme`), and `ThemeProvider`
  reads `data-theme` **only on mount**. A raw `setAttribute('data-theme', …)` in
  Playwright will NOT re-color a mounted globe. The verifiable recolor path is:
  click a theme on `/settings` then navigate to `/travel` in the same session
  (state persists via localStorage), OR set the theme and reload `/travel`. AC 3
  is written so the evaluator uses one of those paths and diffs the canvas region.
- Drag = a canvas mouse drag (`mouse.move` → `mouse.down` → `mouse.move` →
  `mouse.up`). Auto-rotate / drag / reduce-motion are confirmed by frame-diff of
  the canvas region between screenshots, not by reading code.
- This sprint may leave the old below-globe label and Countries list in place
  (removed in Sprint 3); their presence must NOT be used to fail Sprint 1, and
  the globe stage may keep its current fixed height (globe-first layout is
  Sprint 3).

## Agreed acceptance criteria (6)

1. **Filled regions, not dots; outlines preserved.** On `/travel`, the 9 target
   countries render as **filled, area-covering polygons** on the sphere — clearly
   2D regions hugging the surface, not pin dots or just outlines — while **all
   other countries still show their border outlines** so the globe still reads as
   a world map. Verifiable by rotating each target country to face the camera and
   confirming, in centered screenshots at 1280 and 390, a colored area filling
   the country's footprint (a sampled interior pixel is the category color, not
   the sphere/ocean color) sitting on top of the outline map, with no pin-sphere
   primitives remaining.

2. **Fills are on the correct landmasses, in exactly 3 category colors.** Each
   fill sits on its real country (India, United States, and the 7 visited:
   Italy, France, Japan, Croatia, Tanzania, Costa Rica, Turkey), aligned to that
   country's outline — not displaced onto ocean or a neighbor. Coloring is
   **exactly 3 distinct colors by category**: India = Home color, United States =
   Lived color, all 7 visited countries = the single Visited color. The three
   sampled fill colors are clearly distinguishable from each other AND from the
   sphere color (perceptible delta in sampled pixels). Verifiable by sampling
   interior pixels of India, the US, and ≥2 visited countries after rotating each
   into view.

3. **Large countries are complete and clean (no triangulation defects).** The
   United States and India — the triangulation stress cases — render as
   **complete, correctly-shaped fills**: the mainland footprint is filled edge to
   edge with **no gaping holes, no spike/needle slivers shooting off the
   landmass, and no fill cutting visibly through/under the sphere** (the fill
   curves with the surface, never disappearing behind the globe on the near
   hemisphere). Verifiable by centering each and inspecting the silhouette in
   1280 and 390 screenshots: the colored region matches the country outline with
   no stray geometry outside it and no obvious missing wedge inside it.

4. **Theme-aware live recolor, legible in all 3 themes.** Switching theme (via
   `/settings`, then `/travel`, in-session — or set-theme-then-reload) recolors
   the category fills with **no manual reload of the globe component**, and in
   each of light, dark, and sunset the three category fills remain
   distinguishable from each other and from the sphere. Verifiable by sampling
   the same India / US / visited interior pixels in all 3 themes and confirming
   the sampled colors change across themes and stay mutually + sphere-distinct
   (screenshots in all 3 themes).

5. **Globe behaviors preserved (auto-rotate + reduce-motion gate + drag).** With
   reduce-motion off, the globe **auto-rotates on load** (two canvas screenshots
   ~1s apart, no user input, differ in the canvas region) and a **drag rotates
   it** (screenshot before vs after a canvas mouse drag differ). With
   `data-reduce-motion="true"` (set via `/settings` toggle or reload), the globe
   **does not auto-spin** (two screenshots ~1s apart with no input are
   effectively identical in the canvas region) but a **drag still rotates it**
   (before/after differ). All four observations are frame-diffed.

6. **Gates.** `pnpm build` exits 0 (static export succeeds); **zero console /
   page errors** on `/travel` (and the homepage, for regression) during load and
   the interactions above; **no horizontal overflow** at 390px on `/travel`
   (`document.documentElement.scrollWidth <= window.innerWidth`); and no broken
   images (`naturalWidth > 0`) on `/travel`.

## Rationale — one line per change from the DRAFT

- **Draft 1 → AC 1:** Kept "filled not dots + outlines preserved + hugs sphere"
  but made it verifiable by sampling an interior pixel against the sphere color
  and requiring no pin primitives remain, so "filled" is proven, not eyeballed.
- **Draft 2 → AC 2 (strengthened, merged with correctness):** Added the GOAL/SPEC
  requirement that fills land on the **correct landmasses aligned to outlines**
  (the draft never checked placement), and pinned the color check to **sampled
  canvas pixels** with a perceptible delta vs sphere and vs each other.
- **Draft 5 → AC 3 (kept, sharpened):** Made the large-country bar explicit about
  the three real failure modes (holes, spikes/slivers, fill cutting through the
  sphere) and tied each to an observable silhouette check, since this is the
  sprint's actual risk.
- **Draft 3 → AC 4 (realism-fixed):** Same intent, but corrected the
  verification path: `ThemeProvider` reads `data-theme` only on mount, so the AC
  now mandates the **`/settings`-then-/travel (or reload)** recolor path and a
  sampled-pixel diff — a raw `setAttribute` would falsely fail an otherwise
  correct build.
- **Draft 4 → AC 5 (kept, made canvas-specific):** Specified that all four
  observations (auto-rotate on/off, drag in both modes) are confirmed by
  **canvas-region frame-diff** with canvas mouse drags, removing ambiguity about
  how "rotates" is proven.
- **Draft 6 → AC 6 (kept, plus rubric gates folded in):** Added the rubric's
  zero-console-errors-on-homepage regression and broken-image check alongside the
  390px-overflow + build-green gates, so all hard gates that apply to this sprint
  live in one criterion. Stayed at the ~6 cap (no separate AC added).
- **Cut:** No standalone "filled hugging sphere" criterion separate from large
  countries — the through-the-sphere/hug concern is covered once in AC 1
  (general) and stressed in AC 3 (large countries), avoiding redundancy.
