## Acceptance criteria

### AC1 — Filled regions, not dots; outlines preserved — PASS
The 9 targets render as filled area-covering polygons hugging the sphere; all
other countries keep their border outlines (visible grey vector borders across
every screenshot). India centered = a solid red region filling the subcontinent
footprint (`india-centered-light-1280.png`, 5508+ matched fill px); US centered
= a solid blue region filling the contiguous US (`us-centered-light-1280.png`,
12556 matched fill px). Interior pixel samples are the category color, NOT the
sphere: e.g. light India interior (217,41,50) vs sphere (145,157,172),
delta 183; light US interior (51,110,214) vs sphere, delta 113. No pin-sphere
primitives present. Evidence: `probe-travel-default-1280.png`,
`india-centered-light-1280.png`, `us-centered-light-1280.png`, `travel-390.png`.

### AC2 — Correct landmasses, exactly 3 category colors — PASS
Fills sit on the real countries aligned to their outlines (India on the
subcontinent, US on contiguous US, Costa Rica + French Guiana(=France overseas,
expected) green, Italy/Croatia/Tanzania/Japan green patches visible when rotated
in). Exactly 3 colors by category. Light sampled interiors:
Home/India (217,41,50), Lived/US (51,110,214), Visited (45,175,91). Pairwise
distinct: home-lived 243, home-visited 222, lived-visited 139; and each distinct
from sphere (home 183, lived 113, visited 130). Visited confirmed as a single
shared color across multiple visited countries (same green found on Costa
Rica/French Guiana and the Mediterranean/Africa/Japan patches). Evidence:
sampled RGBs above; `india-centered-light-1280.png`, `us-centered-light-1280.png`.

### AC3 — Large countries complete & clean (no triangulation defects) — PASS
US and India (the stress cases) render as complete, correctly-shaped fills.
US silhouette in `us-centered-light-1280.png`: full contiguous-US footprint
filled edge to edge, recognizable outline, no gaping holes, no spike/needle
slivers, no fill cutting under/through the sphere on the near hemisphere (12556
contiguous matched px). India silhouette in `india-centered-light-1280.png`:
full subcontinent incl. peninsula and NE, clean edges, no holes/spikes (5508
matched px). Both curve with the surface (the subdivide-to-4deg triangulation
hugs the sphere). Confirmed in all 3 themes (india/us-centered-{theme}-1280.png).

### AC4 — Theme-aware live recolor, legible in all 3 themes — PASS
Seeding `localStorage.theme` + loading `/travel` (the alignment-mandated recolor
path; ThemeProvider reads data-theme on mount) recolors the category fills with
no manual globe reload. Same India/US/visited interior pixels sampled per theme:
- light: home(217,41,50) lived(51,110,214) visited(45,175,91)
- dark:  home(209,119,114) lived(111,159,199) visited(118,187,136)
- sunset:home(192,15,31)  lived(22,82,200)  visited(19,139,62)
Cross-theme sampled deltas prove recolor (home light→dark 101, lived light→dark
79, visited light→dark 87). In every theme the 3 categories stay mutually
distinct (min pairwise: light 139, dark 69, sunset 149) and distinct from the
sphere (min: light 113, dark 235, sunset 142). Evidence: `travel-{light,dark,
sunset}-1280.png`, `india-centered-{theme}-1280.png`, `dark-visited-probe.png`.

### AC5 — Globe behaviors (auto-rotate + reduce-motion gate + drag) — PASS
All four observations frame-diffed (mean abs RGB diff over the canvas box):
- reduce-motion OFF, no input, 2.5s apart: diff 1.65 (auto-rotates). 1.2s apart:
  diff 1.20. Both clearly > 0.
- reduce-motion OFF, drag: before/after diff 7.54 (drag rotates).
- reduce-motion ON (data-reduce-motion="true"), no input, 2.5s apart: diff 0.00
  (no auto-spin). 1.2s apart: 0.00.
- reduce-motion ON, drag: before/after diff 7.20 (drag still rotates).
The 0.00 vs 1.20–1.65 contrast cleanly separates auto-rotate-on from -off.
Evidence: `autorotate-A.png`/`autorotate-B.png`, `motion-on/off-t0/t1.png`.

### AC6 — Gates — PASS
Build 0; zero console/page errors on `/travel` and `/` (and `/about`); no 390px
overflow on `/travel` (390==390); no broken images on `/travel`. See hard gates.
